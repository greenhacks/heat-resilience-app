Resilience Resources

Actions

By signing up for heat alerts sent straight to your cell phone, you are already improving your resilience to heat!

Here are some more ideas:

1. Have conversations. Conversations have been shown to increase resilience because they sow seeds for preparedness and action. You can increase your personal and community resilience simultaneously by establishing a dialogue with your friends, family, and loved ones about extreme climate conditions. Try these conversation starters: "Have you noticed that summers have started earlier and have gotten hotter?" "How do you feel about the changing climate?" "How prepared do you feel? Is there something we can do together?"
2. Check out urban cooling centers near you. Cities like Los Angeles and Miami are recognizing the impacts of heat. Miami has even hired its first Chief Heat Officer in 2021! You can learn more about solutions for urban heat here: https://www.cdc.gov/climateandhealth/docs/UseOfCoolingCenters.pdf
3. Understand the impacts of heat. Climate Resolve's Rx for Hot Cities: https://www.climateresolve.org/rx-for-hot-cities/
